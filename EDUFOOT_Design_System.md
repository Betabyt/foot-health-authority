# EDU-FOOT Design System

**Version 1.0 — March 2026**
*Every Diabetic Ulcer Footcare-Oriented Outcome Training*

---

## 1. Brand Overview

EDU-FOOT NPC is a South African non-profit company focused on eliminating preventable diabetic foot amputations through competency-based nurse training, standardised screening protocols, and clinic-ready toolkits. The brand must communicate clinical credibility, urgency, accessibility, and hope — bridging the worlds of healthcare education, public health systems, and corporate/funder partnerships.

**Brand Tagline:** *Step Ahead of Amputation*

**Brand Acronym Expansion:** Every Diabetic Ulcer Footcare-Oriented Outcome Training

**Tone of Voice:** Authoritative yet accessible. Clinical precision balanced with human empathy. Data-driven but story-led. The brand speaks to nurses, funders, government officials, and patients — each with clarity and warmth.

## 2. Logo & Brand Mark

### Primary Logo
The EDU-FOOT logo combines a **shield/heart shape** in blue with a **footprint icon** featuring a green leaf accent. The wordmark "EDU-FOOT" sits alongside in bold sans-serif type.

### Logo Variants
- **Full lockup:** Icon + wordmark + tagline ("Step Ahead of Amputation")
- **Compact lockup:** Icon + wordmark (no tagline)
- **Icon only:** Shield/heart with footprint — for favicons, app icons, social avatars
- **Wordmark only:** For inline document use where the icon is impractical

### Clear Space
Minimum clear space around the logo should equal the height of the "E" in EDU-FOOT on all sides. Never place the logo on visually busy backgrounds without a container or backdrop.

### Minimum Size
- Print: 25mm wide (full lockup)
- Digital: 120px wide (full lockup)
- Icon only: 32px minimum

### Misuse
- Do not stretch, rotate, or distort the logo
- Do not change the colour relationships
- Do not add drop shadows, outlines, or effects
- Do not place on low-contrast backgrounds without a container

## 4. Typography

### Primary Typeface — Headings & Display

**Font:** `Montserrat` (Google Fonts — open source)

Montserrat is geometric, modern, and highly legible at large sizes. It projects professionalism and confidence appropriate for healthcare and education contexts.

| Style | Weight | Size | Line Height | Usage |
|-------|--------|------|-------------|-------|
| Display | Bold (700) | 36–48px | 1.1 | Hero headlines, cover titles |
| H1 | Bold (700) | 28–32px | 1.2 | Page/section titles |
| H2 | SemiBold (600) | 22–24px | 1.25 | Subsection headers |
| H3 | SemiBold (600) | 18–20px | 1.3 | Card titles, feature headers |
| H4 | Medium (500) | 16–18px | 1.35 | Sub-headers, label titles |

### Secondary Typeface — Body & UI

**Font:** `Source Sans 3` (Google Fonts — open source)

Source Sans is optimised for readability in long-form text and UI elements. It works well at small sizes and across screen densities — critical for mobile-first clinic tools.

| Style | Weight | Size | Line Height | Usage |
|-------|--------|------|-------------|-------|
| Body Large | Regular (400) | 18px | 1.6 | Lead paragraphs, key statements |
| Body | Regular (400) | 16px | 1.6 | Standard body copy |
| Body Small | Regular (400) | 14px | 1.5 | Captions, metadata, secondary text |
| Label | SemiBold (600) | 14px | 1.4 | Form labels, button text, badges |
| Micro | Medium (500) | 12px | 1.4 | Footnotes, legal text, timestamps |

### Monospace (Data/Code)

**Font:** `Source Code Pro` — for data tables, reference numbers, and any clinical data displays.

### Print Materials

For printed toolkits, protocol cards, and posters where Google Fonts are unavailable, substitute with:
- Headings: **Arial Bold** or **Helvetica Bold**
- Body: **Arial Regular** or **Helvetica Regular**

### Typography Rules
- Maximum line length: 65–75 characters for optimal readability
- Paragraph spacing: 1em between paragraphs (no indentation)
- Never use all-caps for body text; reserve for short labels and badges only
- Headings should use sentence case (not Title Case) in running content — except for formal document titles and branded terms

## 5. Iconography

### Style
- **Line icons** with 2px stroke weight at 24px base size
- Rounded caps and joins for warmth and approachability
- Consistent with Lucide, Phosphor, or similar open-source icon sets
- Custom icons for the ASK-LOOK-FEEL-TEST-ACT protocol steps should follow the same stroke style

### Protocol Step Icons

| Step | Icon Concept | Colour |
|------|-------------|--------|
| ASK | Question mark / speech bubble | `#0072BC` Blue |
| LOOK | Eye / magnifying glass | `#0072BC` Blue |
| FEEL | Hand / touch gesture | `#0072BC` Blue |
| TEST | Monofilament / testing device | `#0072BC` Blue |
| ACT | Alert triangle / action arrow | Risk-coloured (Green/Yellow/Orange/Red) |

### General Icons
- Use filled variants sparingly — only for high-emphasis contexts (active nav states, selected items)
- Maintain 4px padding within icon containers
- Icons accompanying text should be vertically centred with the first line

## 7. Component Library

### Cards

Cards are the primary content container across both digital and presentation contexts.

**Standard Card:**
- Background: White (`#FFFFFF`)
- Border: 1px solid `#E0E0E0` or no border with `box-shadow: 0 2px 8px rgba(0,0,0,0.08)`
- Border-radius: 12px
- Padding: 24px
- Optional: colour-coded top bar (4px height) using primary or accent colours

**Stat Card (used for KPIs like "92%", "R2,500", "10:1"):**
- Large number in EDU-FOOT Blue or risk colour, Montserrat Bold, 36–48px
- Supporting label below in Source Sans Regular, 14px, Text Secondary
- Left-side colour accent bar (4px wide) for visual grouping

**Risk Card:**
- Uses risk classification colour as full top border (4px)
- Icon + risk level label as header
- Action items as body content

### Buttons

| Type | Style | Usage |
|------|-------|-------|
| Primary | Filled, `#0072BC`, white text, 12px radius | Main actions, CTAs |
| Secondary | Outlined, `#0072BC` border, blue text | Alternative actions |
| Success | Filled, `#4CAF50`, white text | Confirm, complete |
| Danger | Filled, `#F44336`, white text | Urgent referral, destructive |
| Ghost | No fill/border, blue text | Tertiary actions, inline links |

- Height: 44px minimum (touch target compliance)
- Padding: 12px 24px
- Font: Source Sans SemiBold, 14–16px
- Disabled state: 40% opacity, no pointer events

### Tables

- Header row: `#E8F4FD` background, Montserrat SemiBold 14px
- Body rows: alternating white / `#F5F5F5`
- Cell padding: 12px 16px
- Border: 1px solid `#E0E0E0` between rows
- Responsive: horizontally scrollable on mobile with sticky first column

### Forms

- Input height: 44px
- Border: 1px solid `#E0E0E0`, focus state 2px `#0072BC`
- Border-radius: 8px
- Label: Source Sans SemiBold 14px, positioned above input
- Error text: `#D32F2F`, 12px, positioned below input with error icon

### Badges / Tags

- Risk badges: pill-shaped, filled with risk colour, white text
- Status badges: outlined with colour border, coloured text
- Height: 24px, padding 4px 12px, font 12px SemiBold

## 8. Data Visualisation

### Chart Colour Sequence

For multi-series charts, use this ordered palette:

1. `#0072BC` — EDU-FOOT Blue
2. `#4CAF50` — EDU-FOOT Green
3. `#FF9800` — Orange accent
4. `#F44336` — Red accent
5. `#1A3A5C` — Dark Blue
6. `#FFC107` — Yellow accent

### Bar Charts (Comparison Data)
- Rounded top corners (4px radius)
- Minimum bar width: 32px
- Value labels above bars in Montserrat SemiBold
- Axis labels in Source Sans Regular 12px

### Before/After Comparisons
A recurring pattern across EDU-FOOT materials (e.g., screening coverage baseline vs. post-training). Use:
- Side-by-side bars with clear colour differentiation
- Percentage change annotation between bars
- Baseline in muted colour (`#999999`), post-training in EDU-FOOT Blue or Green

### Pie / Donut Charts (Revenue Mix, Fund Allocation)
- Donut preferred over pie (better readability)
- Centre label for total or primary metric
- Legend below chart, horizontal layout
- Maximum 5–6 segments; group smaller items into "Other"

### Timelines / Roadmaps
- Horizontal for desktop, vertical for mobile
- Phase markers with colour-coded headers (Phase 1 = Green, Phase 2 = Blue, Phase 3 = Orange)
- Milestone dots with connecting lines

## 10. Photography & Imagery

### Style Direction
- **Authentic:** Real clinical settings, real healthcare workers, real South African contexts
- **Warm and professional:** Well-lit, natural tones, not sterile or cold
- **Action-oriented:** Show nurses screening, training in progress, hands-on practice
- **Inclusive:** Reflect the diversity of South African healthcare workers and patients
- **Hopeful:** Focus on prevention and empowerment, not suffering or trauma

### Image Treatment
- Rounded corners (12px) when used in cards or containers
- Blue overlay at 15% opacity for hero/banner images to maintain brand consistency
- Never use stock photography that looks generic or Western-clinical-only
- Prefer images showing the screening protocol in action, monofilament testing, and group training

### Illustrations
- Simple, flat-style medical illustrations for protocol diagrams
- Colour-coded by protocol step or risk level
- Used in toolkits, posters, and laminated cards where photography is impractical

## 11. The ASK-LOOK-FEEL-TEST-ACT Visual System

This is the most critical visual asset in the EDU-FOOT brand. The 5-step protocol must be instantly recognisable and learnable across all formats.

### Visual Format
- **Vertical flowchart** with downward arrows connecting each step
- Each step is a rounded rectangle with:
  - Step icon (left)
  - Step name in bold (header)
  - Checklist items (body)
- Colour coding: each step can use a unique accent from the brand palette, or all can use EDU-FOOT Blue with the ACT step dynamically coloured by risk outcome

### Adaptations
- **Poster (A2/A1):** Full flowchart with detailed checklist items, laminated for clinic wall display
- **Protocol card (A5):** Condensed version, laminated, pocket-sized for nurse reference
- **Digital (mobile):** Interactive stepped form in WhatsApp or web app
- **Training slide:** Animated reveal, one step at a time
- **Patient-facing:** Simplified visual with pictograms for health literacy

### Risk Classification Matrix

| Risk Level | Colour | Action | Follow-up |
|-----------|--------|--------|-----------|
| Green | `#4CAF50` | Continue self-care education | Annual screening |
| Yellow | `#FFC107` | Enhanced monitoring + footwear review | 6-month follow-up |
| Orange | `#FF9800` | Active wound care + podiatry referral | 3-month follow-up |
| Red | `#F44336` | Urgent specialist referral within 24h | Immediate |

## 12. Digital Product Patterns

### WhatsApp Integration
- Referral templates use structured text with emoji markers for risk levels (🟢🟡🟠🔴)
- Photo guidance follows a standardised format: dorsal view, plantar view, close-up of concern
- Messages should be concise, structured, and actionable

### Mobile Screening Form
- Single-question-per-screen flow for speed
- Large touch targets (minimum 44px)
- Risk colour feedback at completion
- Offline-capable (critical for low-connectivity clinic environments)
- Auto-save progress

### Dashboard (Admin/M&E)
- KPI cards at top: nurses trained, screenings completed, referrals made, amputation rate
- Filterable by district, facility, date range
- Chart types: bar (comparisons), line (trends), donut (distributions)
- Export to PDF for reporting to funders

## 13. Print Production Guidelines

### Toolkit Materials
- **Monofilament packaging label:** EDU-FOOT branding, usage instructions, expiry tracking
- **Laminated protocol card:** Waterproof, wipe-clean, A5 landscape
- **Risk poster:** A2 portrait, high-contrast for clinic wall visibility at 2m distance
- **Screening form pad:** A5, tear-off, carbon-copy or single-use
- **Referral template:** A5, pre-printed fields, fax/WhatsApp compatible

### Paper & Finish
- Protocol cards and posters: 350gsm, matte lamination
- Forms: 80gsm uncoated (writable)
- Business proposal covers: 250gsm, soft-touch lamination

### Colour Notes
- Print in CMYK; request Pantone matching for risk classification colours on high-volume runs
- Ensure risk colours remain distinguishable under fluorescent clinic lighting

## 14. Accessibility Standards

- **WCAG 2.1 AA** compliance for all digital interfaces
- Minimum touch target: 44×44px
- Never rely on colour alone — always pair with text labels, icons, or patterns
- Alt text on all images
- Logical heading hierarchy for screen readers
- Support for multilingual content (English, Zulu, Sotho, Afrikaans at minimum)
- Print materials designed for readability at arm's length (minimum 12pt body text)
